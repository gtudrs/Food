Feijoada

Ingredients:

  • 500 g dried black beans (or 2 cans, drained and rinsed)
  • 1.2 kg assorted meats, such as:
    • 300 g smoked sausage (chorizo or linguiça), sliced
    • 300 g pork ribs, cut into pieces
    • 300 g beef brisket or salted beef (carne seca), cubed
    • 300 g smoked bacon, diced
  • 2 tbsp (30 ml) olive oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp (5 g) smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp (2 g) black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp (2 g) cumin (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1.5 L water or beef broth
  • 1 orange (halved, optional, helps reduce fat taste)

For Serving:

  • White rice
  • Farofa (toasted cassava flour, optional)
  • Orange slices (for balance)
  • Chopped coriander or parsley

Steps:

  • Step 1: Prepare the Beans: If using dried beans, soak them overnight in cold water. Drain and rinse before cooking. If using canned beans, skip this step.
  • Step 2: Brown the Meats: In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add bacon and cook until crispy (about 5 minutes). Add sausage, ribs, and beef. Brown for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally. Remove the meats and set aside.
  • Step 3: Cook the Aromatics: In the same pot, sauté onions for 3 minutes until soft. Add garlic, smoked paprika, and cumin. Cook for 30 seconds.
  • Step 4: Simmer the Feijoada: Return the meats to the pot. Add the black beans, bay leaves, and water (or broth). Place the halved orange in the pot (optional, helps cut the fat taste). Bring to a boil, then reduce to a gentle simmer. Cover and cook for 2.5 to 3 hours, stirring occasionally.
  • Step 5: Adjust Seasoning: Once the beans are tender, remove the orange halves and bay leaves. Taste and adjust salt and pepper as needed. If the feijoada is too thick, add a bit of water. If too thin, let it simmer uncovered for 15 more minutes.
  • Step 6: Serve & Garnish: Serve hot with white rice, farofa, and orange slices. Garnish with chopped parsley or coriander.
