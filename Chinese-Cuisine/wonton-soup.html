Wonton Soup

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 lb (225 g) ground pork
  • 1/2 lb (225 g) shrimp, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp (30 ml) soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp (15 ml) sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp (5 g) ginger, grated
  • 1/4 cup (30 g) chopped green onions
  • 1 package wonton wrappers
  • 4 cups (1 L) chicken broth
  • 2 cups (500 ml) water
  • 1 tbsp (15 ml) rice vinegar
  • 1 tsp (5 ml) fish sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps:

  • Step 1: In a bowl, mix ground pork, finely chopped shrimp, soy sauce, sesame oil, garlic, ginger, and green onions. Stir thoroughly until the mixture is well combined and has a slightly sticky texture. This ensures the filling holds together when wrapped.
  • Step 2: Lay a wonton wrapper on a clean surface. Place a small spoonful of filling in the center. Moisten the edges with water, fold it into a triangle, and press firmly to seal. For a traditional look, bring the two opposite corners together and pinch tightly. Repeat with the remaining wrappers.
  • Step 3: In a large pot, bring the chicken broth and water to a gentle boil. Add rice vinegar and fish sauce to enhance the broth's depth of flavor. Let it simmer for 5 minutes to allow the flavors to blend.
  • Step 4: Gently drop the wontons into the broth one by one, stirring occasionally to prevent them from sticking to the bottom. Simmer for about 5-7 minutes, or until the wontons float and the filling is cooked through. The wrappers should become slightly translucent.
  • Step 5: Taste the broth and adjust the seasoning by adding salt and pepper as needed. You can also add a splash of sesame oil for extra fragrance.
  • Step 6: Serve hot, garnished with extra green onions or a drizzle of sesame oil for a flavorful finishing touch. Enjoy the soup while it's warm!
