Bouillabaisse

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb (450 g) white fish fillets (cod, snapper, or halibut), cut into chunks
  • 1/2 lb (225 g) shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/2 lb (225 g) mussels or clams, cleaned
  • 2 tbsp (30 ml) olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (400 g) diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups (1 L) fish stock
  • 1/2 cup (120 ml) dry white wine
  • 1 tsp (5 g) saffron threads
  • 1 tsp (5 g) dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps:

  • Step 1: Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté for about 5 minutes until soft and translucent. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  • Step 2: Pour in the diced tomatoes and white wine, stirring well. Let the mixture simmer for 5 minutes to allow the flavors to develop.
  • Step 3: Add the fish stock, saffron, thyme, salt, and pepper. Stir well and bring to a gentle boil.
  • Step 4: Reduce the heat to low and add the fish fillets. Let them simmer for 5 minutes without stirring to avoid breaking them.
  • Step 5: Gently add the shrimp, mussels, or clams. Cover the pot and allow them to cook for another 5-7 minutes until the shrimp turns pink and the mussels/clams open.
  • Step 6: Taste the broth and adjust seasoning as needed. If the broth is too strong, add a little more stock or water.
  • Step 7: Serve hot in bowls with crusty French bread for dipping.
