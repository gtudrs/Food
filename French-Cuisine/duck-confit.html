Duck Confit

Ingredients:

  • 4 duck legs
  • 2 tbsp (30 g) coarse salt
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp (15 g) fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 tsp (5 g) black pepper
  • 2 cups (500 g) duck fat
  • 1 bay leaf

Steps:

  • Step 1: Rub the duck legs generously with salt, minced garlic, thyme, and black pepper. Place them in a dish, cover, and refrigerate for at least 12 hours or overnight. This curing process enhances the flavor and texture.
  • Step 2: Preheat the oven to 225°F (110°C). Rinse the duck legs under cold water to remove excess salt and pat them dry with a paper towel.
  • Step 3: In a heavy ovenproof pot, melt the duck fat over low heat. Add the bay leaf for extra aroma.
  • Step 4: Submerge the duck legs in the melted fat, ensuring they are fully covered. Place the pot in the oven and cook slowly for about 3-4 hours, until the meat is tender and easily pulls away from the bone.
  • Step 5: Once cooked, remove the duck legs from the fat and let them cool slightly. At this stage, they can be stored in the fat in the refrigerator for several weeks.
  • Step 6: To serve, heat a skillet over medium-high heat. Sear the duck legs skin-side down until golden and crispy, about 5 minutes.
  • Step 7: Serve hot with roasted potatoes or a fresh salad for a traditional French experience.
