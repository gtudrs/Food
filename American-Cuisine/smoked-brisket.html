Smoked Brisket

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole beef brisket (4-6 kg)
  • 3 tbsp (45 ml) olive oil or yellow mustard (for binding)
  • 2 tbsp (30 g) coarse salt
  • 2 tbsp (30 g) black pepper
  • 1 tbsp (15 g) smoked paprika
  • 1 tbsp (10 g) garlic powder
  • 1 tbsp (10 g) onion powder
  • 1 tbsp (10 g) brown sugar
  • 1 tsp (3 g) cayenne pepper (optional for spice)
  • 250 ml apple cider vinegar or beef broth (for spritzing)

Steps:

  • Step 1: Prepare the Brisket: Remove the brisket from the fridge 1 hour before smoking to bring it to room temperature. Trim excess fat, leaving about 6-8 mm of fat cap to keep it juicy. Pat the brisket dry with paper towels.
  • Step 2: Season the Brisket: Coat the brisket with olive oil or yellow mustard to help the rub stick. In a bowl, mix salt, black pepper, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, brown sugar, and cayenne. Generously coat the brisket with the seasoning mix on all sides.
  • Step 3: Smoking the Brisket: Preheat the smoker to 110-120°C using oak, hickory, or mesquite wood. Place the brisket fat side up on the smoker grate. Smoke for 6-8 hours (about 1.5 hours per kg) until the internal temperature reaches 75°C, spritzing every hour with apple cider vinegar or beef broth.
  • Step 4: Wrapping the Brisket (Texas Crutch): Once the brisket reaches 75°C, wrap it tightly in butcher paper or aluminum foil to retain moisture. Return to the smoker and continue cooking until the internal temperature reaches 92-95°C (this takes another 4-6 hours).
  • Step 5: Resting the Brisket: Remove the brisket from the smoker and let it rest for 1-2 hours in a cooler or wrapped in foil. This allows the juices to redistribute, making the brisket more tender.
  • Step 6: Slicing and Serving: Slice against the grain into 5-10 mm thick pieces. Serve with BBQ sauce, pickles, coleslaw, or fresh bread.
